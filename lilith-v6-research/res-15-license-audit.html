RES-15 — Full Dependency License Audit

Researched: June 28, 2026 · versions checked against PyPI/project docs as of this date

This audit checks whether Lilith v6 can bundle each dependency into a closed-source commercial desktop installer with paid sync. "Commercial OK" means use and redistribution are allowed without a copyleft source-disclosure obligation, subject to license notices. This is not legal advice; red and amber items require owner/legal review before release.

Blockers

  • Qt Quick 3D: RES-01 indicates GPL/commercial licensing creates a blocker for closed-source distribution unless Lilith buys a commercial Qt license or avoids Qt Quick 3D.

Caution Items

  • PySide6: LGPL compliance requires dynamic/replaceable Qt libraries, license texts, and no onefile packaging that prevents replacement.
  • sqlcipher3 / SQLCipher: exact wheel/source license and bundled crypto notices must be audited in the packaging report.
  • Kokoro weights: verify the exact model-weight artifact license before bundling in installers.
  • PyTorch CUDA wheels: if shipped, include NVIDIA/CUDA third-party notices and check redistribution terms for bundled CUDA libraries.
Overall verdict: blocked until Qt Quick 3D licensing is resolved. Most dependencies are permissive and compatible with closed-source commercial distribution. The decisive issue is Qt Quick 3D; the practical path is a commercial Qt license or replacing Qt Quick 3D with a permissively licensed rendering approach.
